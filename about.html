About

Computational Scientist | PhD Researcher | Student of the world

Hi, I'm Hussen Oumer Mohammed, a PhD researcher in computational physics at the Donostia International Physics Center (DIPC) and the University of the Basque Country UPV/EHU.

Most people know me as a computational scientist. But outside of research, I spend a lot of my time learning. I think there's so much beauty in the world, and I want to understand as much of it as I can while I'm here.

I was a physics student before I was a computational physicist. I studied at Uppsala University, where I developed a deep fascination for the intersection of physics, chemistry, and computation. This passion led me to my current work, focusing on molecular dynamics simulations of thermoresponsive ionic liquids.

Right now, I'm transitioning toward AI-driven R&D, because I believe machine learning can unlock new frontiers in molecular design and accelerate scientific discovery. I'm also passionate about open-source and have published several computational tools on GitHub.

This site is a passion project where I collect my original research, code, and ideas. I'm passionately curious about the world and still have a lot to learn.
